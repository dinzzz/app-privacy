2. Scope of This Policy

This policy applies exclusively to the ParallelLives iOS application distributed through the Apple App Store and to any websites we operate that explicitly reference it. It does not apply to third-party services (including the Apple App Store, Apple's in-app purchase system, or any external links) whose privacy practices are governed by their own policies. If there is any inconsistency between this policy and Apple's App Store terms with respect to data Apple controls, Apple's terms prevail for that data.

3. Information You Provide to the App

During profile setup and normal use, you may voluntarily enter the following information, which is stored only on your device in Apple's local storage (UserDefaults and/or on-device database), unless an optional cloud-sync feature is explicitly enabled by you in a future release:

  • A first name or nickname (free-text; you may enter anything, including a pseudonym)
  • An age or age range (used locally to tailor generated content)
  • A career or interest selection (used locally as a seed for life generation)
  • Any custom text or selections you provide within the App
  • Your preferences and in-App settings (theme, audio, notifications)

You are not required to provide real identifying information. You may use a pseudonym. The App does not verify the accuracy of anything you provide, and the Developer is not responsible for consequences arising from information you choose to submit.

4. Information the App Generates on Your Device

As you use the App, it creates data locally, including:

  • Your collection of generated "alternate lives," including their stories, stats, traits, and metadata
  • Gacha mechanics state: pull history, pity counters, merge history, fate card state, timeline chain progress, secret archive fragments, achievements
  • Credit balances, daily free-pull state, and purchase status (kept in sync with Apple's receipts)
  • Timestamps of in-App actions used to enforce daily limits and streaks

All of the above is stored locally on your device. We do not receive a copy on our servers. Deleting or reinstalling the App permanently erases this data.

5. Information Processed by Third Parties on Our Behalf

To deliver specific features, limited data may be transmitted from your device to third-party processors acting on our behalf or under their own responsibility. The following table is exhaustive as of the effective date of this policy:

Apple Inc.

Apple processes your App Store account and in-app purchase information in order to deliver the App and process transactions. We receive only anonymized purchase receipts and subscription status via StoreKit; we do not receive your Apple ID, email, or payment instrument. See Apple's Privacy Policy.

AI Model Providers (optional feature)

When AI-generated content is enabled (for example, to generate a unique alternate life beyond the built-in library), the App sends a small structured prompt to our server endpoint at api.dinzstudio.com, which relays the prompt to a large-language-model provider (currently OpenAI, L.L.C.). The prompt includes only the seed data you entered (for example, first name or nickname, age range, career selection) and parameters needed to shape the response. It does not include your Apple ID, device identifiers, precise location, contacts, photos, or any data other than what is necessary to produce the requested generation. Our server does not persistently store prompts or responses beyond transient operational logs used to prevent abuse and stabilize the service. OpenAI processes content under its API Data Usage Policies; as of the effective date, API content is not used to train OpenAI's models. If this feature is disabled or unavailable in your region, no data is transmitted for this purpose.

Crash and Diagnostic Services (optional)

If enabled, the App may use Apple's built-in crash reporting or a third-party provider (such as Firebase Crashlytics) to capture anonymized crash traces that help us fix defects. Crash payloads contain device model, OS version, anonymized device identifier (reset-able by you in iOS Settings), and a stack trace. They do not contain your collection or profile inputs. You may disable analytics sharing at any time in iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Analytics & Improvements.

6. Information We Do Not Collect

We do not collect, and will never knowingly collect without a clear and separate opt-in:

  • Your real name, email address, phone number, physical address, or government identifier
  • Your contacts, calendar, photos, health data, or messages
  • Your precise or coarse geographic location
  • Your advertising identifier (IDFA) — we do not use advertising and do not serve third-party ads
  • Cross-app or cross-website tracking data
  • Biometric data, face prints, or voice prints
  • Payment card numbers or bank information (Apple handles all payments)

We do not sell, rent, lease, license, or otherwise disclose personal information to data brokers or advertising networks under any circumstances. We do not engage in "targeted advertising" or in the "sharing" of personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising as defined under the CCPA/CPRA.

8. How We Use Information

We use the limited information described above solely to:

Content generated by artificial intelligence is probabilistic and fictional. Outputs do not reflect real people, real events, or factual claims. Occasional outputs may be unexpected, imperfect, or in poor taste despite our safety filtering. We expressly disclaim any warranty regarding the accuracy, appropriateness, or reliability of AI-generated content. Do not submit confidential information, trade secrets, or personal data about identifiable third parties in any free-text field that is sent to an AI provider.

By using AI features, you acknowledge that (a) your prompts are transmitted to an AI model provider as described in Section 5, (b) the provider may retain prompts briefly for abuse monitoring, and (c) AI outputs are provided to you on an "as is" basis with no warranty.

Purchases and subscriptions (including "Portal Credits" and the "Infinite Pass") are processed exclusively by Apple through StoreKit. We do not receive your payment card information. We receive a signed StoreKit receipt that confirms the transaction and, for subscriptions, the current status (active, in grace period, expired). Consumable purchases (for example, credit packs) are generally non-refundable once consumed; refund requests are subject to Apple's discretion under its published policies.

11. Analytics, Crash Reporting, and Diagnostics

At launch, the App ships without third-party advertising or behavioral analytics. We may enable optional, aggregated, privacy-preserving analytics or crash reporting in a future release. Any such change will be disclosed in this policy before activation. You will always be able to decline diagnostics sharing through iOS system settings.

12. Data Retention and Deletion

On-device data persists until you delete the App or reset it. Prompts transmitted to AI providers are transient and are not persistently stored on our servers. Aggregated, non-identifying telemetry (for example, counters) may be retained indefinitely. StoreKit receipt metadata (transaction IDs, product IDs, purchase timestamps) is retained for tax, accounting, and dispute-resolution purposes for the statutory period required under Croatian and EU law (typically up to eleven (11) years for accounting records).

To delete locally stored data, delete the App or use the "Reset All Data" option in Settings. For any data you believe we hold, contact dinz@dinzzz.com and we will confirm within thirty (30) days.
